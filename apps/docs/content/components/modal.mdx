## Overview

Use modals to confirm irreversible actions, collect short form data, or present critical information that requires acknowledgment. Keep content succinct and focused on the decision at hand. For lengthy flows, prefer dedicated pages or stepper patterns instead of stacking modals.

Always provide at least one clear dismissal action. Label destructive or high-risk actions explicitly (for example, “Delete record”).

## UX Guidelines

- Dim the background sufficiently to create visual focus but allow users to maintain context.
- Place primary actions on the right and secondary actions on the left for left-to-right locales.
- Avoid placing scrollable content within the modal body unless necessary; when required, keep headers and footers fixed.

## Accessibility details

The component traps focus while open and restores the previously focused element on close. When modals spawn from a control, ensure the trigger remains in the DOM so focus can return. Screen reader users should hear the headline immediately—supply a concise `headline` or `header` slot. Keep the content keyboard navigable and make sure the Escape key path is testable.
