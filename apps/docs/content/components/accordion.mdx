## Overview

Accordions help keep long-form content skimmable by showing one section at a time. Keep section titles short and descriptiveâ€”people should understand what they gain before expanding.

Reserve accordions for content that benefits from a progressive disclosure pattern (for example FAQs, filter panels, or secondary instructional text). Prefer inline expansion or navigation pages when the content is critical to task completion.

## UX Guidelines

- Limit the number of items to prevent excessive scrolling.
- If very important content is hidden behind an accordion, reconsider whether it should be default-open.
- Provide clear feedback for disabled accordion items and explain why they cannot be opened.

## Accessibility details

The component follows the WAI-ARIA accordion pattern using roving tab index. Ensure that focus order remains logical, especially when inserting custom interactive controls inside accordion panels. If panels contain forms, confirm that validation states are announced even when the parent item collapses.
