## Overview

Buttons enable people to act on the current page or commit a task. Choose variants intentionally: use the primary button for the most important action on the view and secondary or ghost variants for less prominent actions.

Place destructive actions away from primary CTAs and communicate the outcome in the label (for example, “Delete project”). Avoid using buttons for navigation between pages—prefer links unless the action commits data.

## UX Guidelines

### Dos

- Keep labels short, specific, and outcome oriented (verb + noun works well).
- Use one primary button per surface to avoid competing actions.
- Group related buttons and maintain consistent spacing between them.

### Don’ts

- Don’t mix different button sizes within the same group.
- Don’t rely on color alone to communicate destructive actions—pair with iconography or explicit labels.
- Don’t disable buttons without also communicating why they cannot be used.

## Accessibility details

Buttons follow the WAI-ARIA authoring practices for command buttons. Ensure the focus ring remains visible against both light and dark themes and that contrast between text and fill meets WCAG 2.2 AA (4.5:1).

When introducing toggle behavior, set `aria-pressed` and ensure the state change is announced to assistive technology. If the button triggers a dialog, move focus into the dialog container after activation.
